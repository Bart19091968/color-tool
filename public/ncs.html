About the NCS System

The Natural Colour System (NCS) is a perceptual color model developed in Sweden and based on how humans naturally perceive color. It is the official color standard in Sweden and Norway and widely used in Spain, South Africa and beyond.

NCS notation describes colors using blackness, chromaticness and hue. For example: NCS S 2030-R90B means:
S = Second Edition · 20 = 20% blackness · 30 = 30% chromaticness · R90B = hue (Red 90% towards Blue).

NCS is especially popular in interior design and architecture because it describes colors in a way that matches human perception, making it easier to predict how colors will look in different lighting conditions.

How to read NCS notation

NCS S 2030-R90B decoded:

  • S = Second Edition (standard edition)
  • 20 = Blackness 20% (how much black in the color)
  • 30 = Chromaticness 30% (color intensity/saturation)
  • R90B = Hue: Red, leaning 90% towards Blue = near-blue violet

NCS Hue letters:

  • Y = Yellow · R = Red · B = Blue · G = Green
  • Y30R = Yellow leaning 30% towards Red (orange-yellow)
  • N = Neutral (achromatic greys)
