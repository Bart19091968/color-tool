About RAL Classic

RAL Classic is a European color standard created in Germany in 1927 by the Reichs-Ausschuss für Lieferbedingungen. It is the most widely used color system in European industry, used for paints, powder coatings, plastics and signage.

Each RAL Classic color has a 4-digit code starting with a number indicating its color family: 1xxx = Yellow, 2xxx = Orange, 3xxx = Red, 4xxx = Violet, 5xxx = Blue, 6xxx = Green, 7xxx = Grey, 8xxx = Brown, 9xxx = White/Black.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is RAL 3000 in HEX?
RAL 3000 Flame Red is approximately #AF2B1E in HEX. It is one of the most popular RAL colors, used in safety signage, fire equipment and industrial applications.
What is RAL 7016 Anthracite Grey in HEX?
RAL 7016 Anthracite Grey is approximately #293133. It is extremely popular in architecture and window frames, often used as an alternative to black.
What is the difference between RAL Classic and RAL Design?
RAL Classic has 213 colors with 4-digit codes (RAL 3000). RAL Design System+ has over 1,625 colors organized by hue angle, lightness and chroma — better suited for precise color matching in architecture and interior design. See RAL Design →
How accurate are RAL HEX approximations?
RAL colors are defined for physical pigments. HEX values are widely-published approximations for screen use. Accuracy varies by monitor calibration. For professional coatings, always use physical RAL color fans or fan decks.
