Is Canva Pro Worth It in 2025?

Every feature difference between Free and Pro — and a clear answer on who should actually upgrade.

The Short Answer

Canva Pro is worth it if you design regularly and rely on any of its gated features — particularly background removal, Magic Resize, custom fonts, or transparent PNG exports. If you design occasionally and don't need those tools, the free tier is genuinely capable enough to keep you going indefinitely.

The longer answer requires a closer look at what each plan actually includes and which type of user falls into which camp.

What You Get on the Free Plan

Canva Free is not a stripped-down teaser — it is a fully functional design tool. You get access to over 250,000 templates spanning every common format, a stock library with millions of free photos and graphics, 5 GB of cloud storage, and real-time collaboration with other users. The free plan also includes one Brand Kit (your colours, fonts, and logo), basic video editing, and Canva Docs.

For a student creating a presentation, a small business owner making occasional flyers, or a hobbyist designing social posts, the free plan covers nearly everything needed. Many users run on it for years without feeling constrained.

What Canva Pro Adds

Background Remover

One of Pro's most-used features. A single click removes the background from any photo — no manual masking required. The results are good enough for most product photography and social content, though complex hair or fur edges can still trip it up. If you regularly create product shots, headshots, or sticker-style graphics, this alone can justify the Pro price.

Magic Resize

Magic Resize converts a finished design to any other format in one click — turn an Instagram square into a Story, a Facebook cover, a LinkedIn post, and a YouTube thumbnail without rebuilding each one from scratch. For social media managers publishing across multiple platforms, the time savings are substantial.

Brand Kit (Unlimited)

While free users get one Brand Kit, Pro unlocks unlimited Brand Kits. This is primarily useful for agencies or freelancers managing multiple clients — each brand's colours, fonts, and logos can be stored separately and swapped in instantly.

Custom Font Upload

Pro users can upload any typeface as an OTF or TTF file. If your brand guidelines specify a proprietary font, this is a non-negotiable feature. Without it, you are limited to Canva's built-in library — broad, but not guaranteed to include your specific typeface.

Premium Templates and Stock Assets

Pro unlocks an additional tier of premium templates (bringing the total to over 420,000) and grants access to a vastly expanded stock library of over 100 million photos, videos, audio tracks, and graphics. The free stock library is large, but Pro's selection is noticeably higher quality at the premium end.

Storage: 1 TB

Free users get 5 GB. Pro users get 1 TB. If you work with video content or manage large libraries of brand assets, the storage jump is meaningful.

Advanced Export Options

Pro unlocks transparent PNG export (essential for logos and overlays), SVG export, PDF Print quality for professional printing, and CMYK colour profiles. These are important for anyone producing work destined for print or brand asset libraries.

Social Media Scheduling

Pro includes a built-in social scheduler that lets you publish directly from Canva to up to 8 platforms. It is not as fully featured as dedicated tools like Buffer or Later, but for straightforward scheduling it removes the need for a separate subscription.

Who Should Upgrade to Pro

  • Agencies and freelancers managing multiple brand identities who need unlimited Brand Kits and custom font support.
  • Social media managers publishing across several platforms daily — Magic Resize and the scheduler pay for themselves in hours saved.
  • E-commerce sellers who need clean product image backgrounds removed at scale.
  • Anyone producing print materials who needs transparent PNG, PDF Print, or CMYK export.
  • Teams with heavy storage needs — video editors, brand asset managers — who will exhaust the 5 GB free tier quickly.

Who Can Stay on Free

  • Occasional designers who create a few posts or documents per month and don't need Pro-exclusive exports.
  • Students — Canva for Education (free) covers most school and university design needs with many Pro-equivalent features.
  • Personal projects — birthday invitations, personal social posts, hobby blogs — rarely require Pro tools.
  • Anyone trialling Canva for the first time — start free, identify which Pro features would save you time, then upgrade if the case is clear.

Verdict

At around €10/month on the annual plan, Canva Pro is priced fairly for professional use. The four headline features — background removal, Magic Resize, custom fonts, and transparent PNG export — are the honest test: if you need any two of those regularly, the subscription pays for itself in time saved. If you don't, the free tier will serve you well.
